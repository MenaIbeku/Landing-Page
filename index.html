Hi, I'm Mena Ibeku

And this is more about ME!

EDUCATION

I am a student of the University of Massachusetts at Amherst.

With an Informatics major and a concentration in Data Science

Professional Experience & Development

    As a Data Analytics Research Intern at UMass Amherst Cranberry Station since June 2024, I have been leveraging multispectral and infrared drones to gather comprehensive data on cranberry plant growth, development, and nutritional factors. Utilizing QGIS and AgiSoft Metashape for data analysis and visualization, I identify key factors influencing growth rates to aid strategic decision-making, significantly improving cranberry crop production. Additionally, I deploy sensor technology to determine nutritional data, assisting Massachusetts cranberry farmers in optimizing crop production and guiding senior management decisions for enhanced agricultural productivity.

    As a Full Stack Development Intern at the National Center for Technology and Dispute Resolution from September 2023 to January 2024, I utilized Flutter to develop a disaster response app for medical responders, with a focus on continuous improvement and modification. I implemented solutions to enhance communication efficiency in low connectivity areas, improving disaster response coordination. Additionally, I developed and integrated geolocation services to identify support hotspots efficiently and supported the development of applications aimed at enhancing security-based community resilience efforts.

    As a Data Analytics Intern at Niger Delta Power Holding Company in July 2021, I collected, recorded, and analyzed power distribution and sales data using MS Excel and Access. I represented the data in visual formats, which were utilized to support senior management in daily presentations.

Leadership and Community Service

    As the New England Zone Treasurer for the National Society of Black Engineers since May 2024, I spearhead fundraising initiatives and pitch ideas to potential sponsors, ensuring robust financial support. I also manage and monitor budgets, ensuring the organization adheres to financial constraints while planning events.

    As Treasurer of Umass Amherst Brotherly Union since May 2024, I create, manage, and monitor budgets to ensure financial adherence while planning events. I also promote support and empowerment of People of Colour on campus through various activities and workshops hosted by the organization.

    As a volunteer with the UMass Amherst CICS Community Outreach Student Team since October 2023, I promote and enhance outreach efforts in computer science and technology for youth and educators. I also empower students to make a positive impact within their community through occasional workshops.
